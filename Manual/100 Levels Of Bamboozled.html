On the Subject of 100 Levels Of Bamboozled

Good luck.

This module presents you with six octagonal buttons with four LEDs between them. The large display will be cycling English words.

Note that the entire submission is calculated upon entering the last letter.

When you are doing a mission in the "100 Levels of Bamboozled" pack, follow the instruction under "During a Level". Otherwise, follow the instructions under "Outside of a Level".

During a Level

The module will initially cycle the phrase "Welcome to: Level X", where X is the current level.

Upon solving a set portion of the bomb, the bottom middle button will turn green and say "GO". Press it to begin.

Use the final word from the previous level as your starting word. If this is Level 1, use "GOODLUCK" instead.

Use the steps below in order to encrypt your starting word. Only use a step if its condition is true.

Outside of a Level

Use "MINUSWORLD" as your starting word.

Use the steps below in order to encrypt your starting word. Always apply every step.

Step 1: Initial Encryption

Convert each letter of the starting word to a number, using A=0, B=1, ... Z=25.

Multiply each number by 7, then modulo each number by 26.

Convert each number back into a letter, using A=25, B=24, ... Z=0.

If any displayed phrases are wrapped with double quotes, append the letters of those phrases to the new word, in order.

If any displayed phrases are not wrapped with double quotes, and appear in the table below, perform the indicated instructions below, in the order of the phrases.

Phrase Instruction
ALFA Remove the first letter from your word.
ALPHA Remove the last letter from your word.
QUEBEC Reverse your word.
QUEBEK Remove the middle letter from your word. If there is none, remove the two middle letters instead.
CUEBEC Encrypt your word in an Atbash Cipher.
WHISKEY Move the rightmost two letters from your word two spaces to the left, wrapping around if needed.
WHISKY Move the leftmost two letters from your word two spaces to the right, wrapping around if needed.
WISKEY Move every letter from your word two spaces to the left, wrapping around if needed.

Step 2: Grid Encryption

Repeat your word from the previous step 17 times.

Lay out your repeated word in a grid, where each row is 7 letters wide. Lay the letters out in reading order.

Discard the last row if it isn't complete.

Your new word is the rightmost column of the grid, read from bottom to top.

Apply this step only if the
level number is a multiple of 3.

Step 3: Caesar Encryption

Convert each letter of the word from the previous step to a number, using A=1, B=3, ... Z=51.

Take the sum of those numbers, and add the sum of every digit in the bomb's serial number. Caesar shift every letter in the previous step's word forward by this amount. This is your new word.

Apply this step only if the
bomb has a needy module.

Step 4: Needy Encryption

Take the number of currently active needy modules. Duplicate that many letters of the previous step's word in the following fashion:

  1. Take the first letter.
  2. Insert a copy of it after it.
  3. Move on to the next letter, if applicable.

Step 7: Color Encryption

Take the colors of the six buttons in reading order, where red=1, green=2, and blue=3.

Concatenate the digits together and repeat them until they are as long as or longer than the previous step's word. Truncate the result to be exactly the same length by removing digits from the right.

Assosiate the letters of the previous step's word with the digits in the number obtained, where the first letter and first digit correspond.

Step 8: Submission

Take the first eight letters of the word from the previous step. If there are fewer than eight letters, use all of them. This is this level's final word.

Use the button with a left arrow to move to the previous set of buttons. Use the button with a right arrow to move to the next set of buttons.

Press the button corresponding to each letter of your final word in order.

Upon a complete submission, the LEDs will light up. Each LED represents 2 letters submitted. If the LED is red, both were incorrect. If the LED is yellow, one was incorrect. If the LED is green, both were correct.

Determining a Button's Letter

Each button corresponds to a letter.

  • If the button has a single letter on it, that is the letter it corresponds to.
  • If the button has a single letter wrapped in double quotes on it, the letter it corresponds to is that letter encrypted using an Atbash cipher.
  • If the button has a single letter wrapped in single quotes on it, the letter it corresponds to is that letter encrypted using a Rot13 cipher.
  • If the button has a correctly spelled NATO phonetic alphabet letter on it, the letter it corresponds to is that letter.
  • If the button has an incorrectly spelled NATO phonetic alphabet letter on it, do not press that button.
